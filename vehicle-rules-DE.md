## 1. THE THREE COMPLETELY SEPARATE CGT REGIMES

Germany is unique in having three entirely different capital gains regimes depending on asset type. Most people know only Abgeltungsteuer. The other two are fundamentally different.

| Regime | Assets | Rate | Holding period matters? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Abgeltungsteuer | Stocks, bonds, ETFs, funds, dividends, interest | 26.375% flat | NO — same rate regardless |
| Spekulationssteuer | Real estate (investment) | Marginal income rate (up to 47.48%) | YES — after 10 years: tax-free |
| Private Verkaufsgeschäft | Crypto, physical gold | Marginal income rate (up to 47.48%) | YES — after 12 months: tax-free |

## 3. REGIME 1: ABGELTUNGSTEUER — FINANCIAL ASSETS

### What It Covers

All capital income from financial instruments:
- Dividends from stocks and funds
- Interest from bonds, savings accounts, P2P loans
- Capital gains from selling stocks, ETFs, bonds, funds
- Distributions from mutual funds

### Rate

| Component | Rate |
|---|---|
| Base tax | 25% |
| Solidarity surcharge | 5.5% of base = 1.375% |
| Church tax (if applicable) | 8–9% of base = 2–2.25% |
| **Total (without church tax)** | **26.375%** |
| Total (with church tax) | ~28.6% |

No distinction between short-term and long-term gains. All financial gains at 26.375%.

### Sparerpauschbetrag (Saver's Allowance)

| Status | Annual allowance |
|---|---|
| Single | **€1,000** |
| Married / Civil partnership | **€2,000** |

**MUST BE ACTIVELY FILED.** Submit Freistellungsauftrag to each broker. Without it, tax is withheld from the first euro of investment income. Can be split across multiple banks up to the total limit.

Trigger T26: If German resident holds investment depot but no Freistellungsauftrag recorded → surface this fact.

### Günstigerprüfung (Favourable Assessment)

If your personal income tax rate is LOWER than 26.375%, you can apply your personal rate to investment income by filing the relevant section in your tax return. Relevant for:
- Students with low income
- Part-time workers
- Retirees with modest pension income

### Teilfreistellung (Partial Exemption for Funds)

Partial exemption reduces the taxable portion of fund gains:

| Fund Type | Equity % | Exemption | Effective Tax on Gains |
|---|---|---|---|
| Equity funds | ≥51% equity | **30% exempt** | 18.46% |
| Mixed funds | ≥25% equity | **15% exempt** | 22.42% |
| Real estate funds | ≥51% RE | **60% exempt** | 10.55% |
| Foreign REIT funds | ≥51% RE | **80% exempt** | 5.28% |
| Other funds | <25% equity, <51% RE | 0% exempt | 26.375% |

Applies to: dividends from funds, distributions, capital gains on sale, AND Vorabpauschale.

### Loss Ring-Fencing

**Critical rule:** Stock (Aktien) losses can ONLY be offset against stock gains. They CANNOT offset:
- Gains from bonds, ETFs, interest (different loss bucket)
- Real estate gains
- Crypto gains
- Salary or other income

**Practical impact:** If you have €5,000 in stock losses and €5,000 in bond/ETF interest gains → you CANNOT net them. You pay tax on the interest and carry the stock losses forward.

To carry losses forward: request a **Verlustbescheinigung** from your broker by December 15. Without it, losses stay at that specific broker.

### Vorabpauschale (Advance Tax on Accumulating Funds)

Applies annually to accumulating ETFs and funds that don't pay distributions.

**Calculation:**
1. Basisertrag = Fund value at start of year × Basiszins × 0.7
2. Basiszins: official rate set annually by Bundesfinanzministerium (was negative 2019-2021, now positive)
3. If fund value increased: tax on Basisertrag (after Teilfreistellung and Sparerpauschbetrag)
4. If fund value fell: Vorabpauschale = zero
5. Basisertrag reduces capital gains on eventual sale (no double taxation)

**Practical:** Your broker calculates and pays this automatically in January. You must have cash in the account. For most standard ETF portfolios, the annual amount is modest.

## 4. REGIME 2: SPEKULATIONSSTEUER — REAL ESTATE

### Investment / Rental Property

| Holding Period | Tax Treatment | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| **< 10 years** | Taxed as income | **Marginal rate — up to 47.48%** |
| **≥ 10 years** | **COMPLETELY TAX-FREE** | **0%** |

This is the most powerful tax fact in German real estate: hold investment property for 10+ years, sell completely tax-free regardless of the gain. No annual tax on unrealised gains (unlike Netherlands).

**The 10-year clock:** starts on the date of purchase (notarial deed signing), ends 10 years + 1 day later.

**Holding period alert:** Fire at 114 months (approaching 10-year mark).

### Owner-Occupied / Primary Residence

**Completely different rule — no 10-year wait required.**

Tax-free if:
- The property was used EXCLUSIVELY by the owner as primary residence for the **entire holding period**, OR
- The property was used by the owner in the **year of sale AND the two preceding calendar years** (3 calendar years minimum, not necessarily full years)

**Example:** Bought 2020, lived in it 2022–2024 sale year → tax-free regardless of holding period.

### Rental Property Rate

Rental income: taxed as income at marginal rate in Box 1 equivalent. Can deduct:
- Mortgage interest
- AfA depreciation (2% p.a. for old buildings, 3% p.a. for new buildings from 2023)
- Maintenance costs
- Management fees

## 5. REGIME 3: CRYPTO AND PRIVATE SALES ASSETS

### Cryptocurrency (Bitcoin, Ethereum, etc.)

| Holding Period | Tax Treatment | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| **< 12 months** | Taxed as income | **Personal income rate — up to 47.48%** |
| **≥ 12 months** | **COMPLETELY TAX-FREE** | **0%** |

Germany has one of the most crypto-friendly tax regimes in the world. One year and you pay nothing, regardless of the gain size.

**The 12-month clock:** Starts from date of acquisition (purchase, mining reward, staking reward). Each acquisition has its own individual clock.

**De minimis:** If total short-term gains < €600 in the calendar year → no filing required. Above €600 → ALL gains taxable (not just the excess).

**Crypto holding period alert:** Fire at 10 months (2 months warning).

### Staking and Mining

- Staking rewards: taxed as income at receipt
- Mining: taxed as income at receipt
- Subsequent sale of staking/mining rewards: starts new 12-month holding period from receipt date
- Staking does NOT extend the holding period of underlying asset

### Physical Gold and Silver

Same rules as crypto: held < 12 months → marginal income rate. Held ≥ 12 months → completely tax-free.

Gold ETFs: treated as financial funds (Abgeltungsteuer applies), NOT as physical gold.

## 6. INVESTMENT VEHICLES — COMPLETE REFERENCE

### German ETF Depot (Brokerage Account)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Tax treatment | Abgeltungsteuer: 26.375% on gains/dividends above €1,000 allowance |
| Best structure | Accumulating ETFs (thesaurierend): growth reinvested, only Vorabpauschale annually |
| UCITS ETFs | EU-regulated. Teilfreistellung applies. US-domiciled ETFs: may have complications |
| Foreign depot | Must declare gains in German tax return (Anlage KAP). German broker: automatic withholding |
| Recommended brokers | Scalable Capital, Smartbroker+, Finanzen.net Zero (low-cost, automatic Abgeltungsteuer handling) |
| Cross-border flag | Exit tax: if leaving Germany with depot > €500k → mark-to-market CGT event |

### Rürup-Rente (Basis-Rente)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Annual deduction | **€30,826** (2026, single). Doubled for couples: €61,652 |
| Deductibility | **100% deductible** against income in Box 1 equivalent |
| Best for | Self-employed without employer pension, high earners wanting large deduction |
| Tax treatment | EET — deductible contribution, tax-deferred growth, taxable payout |
| Access | Annuity from age **62** (minimum — no lump sum option) |
| **Portability** | **CANNOT be transferred, surrendered, or pledged. Completely illiquid until payout.** |
| **Cross-border** | If leaving Germany: Rürup continues, annuity paid from age 62 regardless of country. Taxed in Germany (likely) at source. Complex |
| NRI in Germany | If Indian national in DE with Rürup: consider India DTAA treatment on annuity income |

### Riester-Rente

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Government subsidy | €175/year basic + €185/child/year |
| Deductibility | Up to €2,100/year (including subsidy) |
| Eligibility | Employees subject to statutory pension insurance |
| Tax treatment | EET — deductible contribution, tax-deferred growth, taxable payout |
| Reality check | Poor returns for most investors after costs. Being phased out. Largely irrelevant for mobile professionals |

### bAV (Betriebliche Altersversorgung — Employer Pension)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Contribution | Via salary sacrifice (Entgeltumwandlung). Up to €302/month tax and NIC-free (2026) |
| Employer obligation | Employer must add minimum 15% if employee saves NIC |
| Types | Direktversicherung (most common), Pensionskasse, Pensionsfonds, Direktzusage |
| Portability | **Near impossible to transfer when leaving Germany or changing employer** |
| Vesting | Employer contributions vest after 3 years of service |
| Cross-border exit | On departure from Germany: complex tax issues. Get specialist advice |
| Mobile professionals | Very poor choice for anyone who might leave Germany |

### Statutory Pension (Gesetzliche Rentenversicherung)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Contribution | 18.6% of gross salary (split 50/50 employer/employee) |
| Cap | Contribution ceiling: €96,600/year (2026) |
| Portability | EU portability rights. Outside EU: bilateral social security agreements |
| NRI in Germany | Contributions paid. May not accrue sufficient years for full pension. Check bilateral agreement between Germany and India |

## 7. EXIT TAX (WEGZUGSBESTEUERUNG)

From January 2025:

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Trigger | Leaving Germany with investment fund holdings > **€500,000** |
| Tax event | Mark-to-market capital gains tax as if sold on departure date |
| Rate | Abgeltungsteuer (26.375%) on unrealised gains |
| Timing | Must pay before departure or on filing |
| Deferral | EU/EEA countries: deferral possible with interest |
| Non-EU | No deferral |

**Critical for mobile professionals:** If you've built up a large ETF portfolio in Germany and are planning to relocate, this is a material financial event. Plan departure timing carefully.

## 8. FOREIGN INVESTMENTS IN GERMANY

### US Stocks / ETFs in German Depot

- German broker withholds US dividend tax at 15% (US-Germany DTA)
- German Abgeltungsteuer: 25% × 70% (after equity fund Teilfreistellung) = 17.5% on dividends
- Credit for US 15% withheld → net German additional tax ≈ 2.5% on 70% of dividend
- Effective double taxation largely avoided

### Indian Investments Held by German Resident

- Gains from Indian stocks/MFs in India: Germany taxes these as income from foreign sources
- Abgeltungsteuer applies: 26.375% on gains
- DTAA: India-Germany tax treaty allows credit for Indian tax paid against German liability
- Indian LTCG already paid: claim credit via Anlage AUS on German tax return

### Foreign Broker Accounts

German residents must declare foreign broker accounts on Anlage KAP. German broker automatically handles withholding; foreign broker does not.
