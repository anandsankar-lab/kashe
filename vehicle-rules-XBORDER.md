## SECTION 1: THE FUNDAMENTAL FRAMEWORK

### Citizenship vs Residency — The Most Important Distinction in This Engine

**Tax residency** determines which country's domestic rules apply to your worldwide income. Most countries tax by residency.

**Citizenship** — for ONE country only — means your obligation never goes away regardless of where you live:

> **The United States is the only major economy that taxes its citizens on worldwide income regardless of where they live.**

An Indian citizen living in Amsterdam: Dutch tax rules apply. Indian rules apply only to Indian-source income.
A US citizen living in Amsterdam: Dutch tax rules apply AND US rules apply. Both simultaneously. Forever.

This is not theoretical. It shapes every investment decision a US person makes abroad.

### The Five Countries — How They Determine Tax Residency

| Country | Residency Test |
|---|---|
| India | ≥182 days present in India in the financial year |
| UK | Statutory Residence Test (SRT) — complex multi-factor test. More than 183 days usually sufficient |
| Netherlands | Registered with municipality (inschrijving). Centre of economic interest |
| Germany | Wohnsitz or gewöhnlicher Aufenthalt — residence or habitual abode (>6 months) |
| USA | Green card OR substantial presence (183-day weighted test) OR citizenship |

## SECTION 2: DTAA QUICK REFERENCE — ALL FIVE MARKET PAIRS

### India–Netherlands

| Income Type | Withholding Cap | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Interest | 10% | NRE interest: tax-free in India → included in NL Box 3 |
| Dividends | 10% (≥10% shareholding) / 15% otherwise | |
| Capital gains | Source country taxing rights (property in India taxed in India) | Shares: depends on article |
| Relief method | Credit method in Netherlands | File Form 67 in India for FTC claim |

**Key NRE account issue for Dutch residents:** NRE interest is tax-exempt in India. India provides no withholding. Netherlands cannot give a Foreign Tax Credit because there is no foreign tax. NRE interest is included in Dutch Box 3 at assumed return rate.

### India–United Kingdom

| Income Type | Withholding Cap | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Interest (NRO) | 15% | FTC available in UK for 15% withheld |
| Interest (NRE) | **0% in India** | BUT taxable in UK at up to 40%. No FTC. Tax sparing credit: time-limited |
| Dividends | 10–15% | FTC available in UK |
| Capital gains | Credit method | India taxes Indian gains; UK gives FTC |
| Relief method | Credit method | UK self-assessment: claim FTC |

**The NRE trap for UK residents:** NRE interest is zero-taxed in India because India uses it as an incentive for foreign inward investment. The UK-India DTAA provides a "tax sparing" credit — the UK credits tax as if India had charged 15%, even though India charged nothing. BUT this credit is time-limited (10 years from when remittance basis ended — approximately expires 2035). After expiry: 40% UK tax, no offset.

**FIG window:** New UK residents (first 4 tax years, after 10 years non-UK) may have NRE interest entirely exempt under FIG. File FIG claim by January 31 following the tax year.

### India–United States

| Income Type | US Treatment | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Interest (NRE) | **Taxable in US** despite Indian exemption | DTAA saving clause preserves US right to tax citizens |
| Dividends from Indian stocks | Taxable in US | Credit for Indian withholding |
| LTCG on Indian equity | Taxable in US | Credit for Indian CGT. BUT: PFIC rules may override |
| Indian MFs (PFIC) | Ordinary income rate + interest charges | Form 8621 required annually |
| PPF interest | US tax position unclear | Generally not recognised as pension. IRS has not issued definitive guidance |
| NRE FD interest | Taxable in US | No FTC (India exempt) — full US rate |

**The saving clause:** Article 29 of the US-India DTAA preserves the right of each country to tax its own citizens. A US citizen cannot use the India-US DTAA to avoid US tax on Indian income. The DTAA helps reduce Indian withholding — it does not reduce US tax.

### India–Germany

| Income Type | German Treatment | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| NRE FD interest | Abgeltungsteuer: 26.375% | India exempts it; Germany taxes it |
| Indian equity gains | Abgeltungsteuer on realisation | DTAA credit for Indian CGT paid |
| Indian MF gains | Abgeltungsteuer. No PFIC rule | Germany does not have PFIC-equivalent |
| PPF interest | Potentially taxable as income | Specialist advice essential |
| Indian pension | Complex — DTAA treaty pension article applies | |
| Relief method | Anlage AUS — credit for Indian tax paid | |

**German advantage vs US:** Germany does NOT have a PFIC equivalent. An Indian national in Germany can hold Indian MFs without PFIC complications. Gains taxed at 26.375% (Abgeltungsteuer) on realisation. Much simpler than US treatment.

### Netherlands–United Kingdom

Primarily relevant when user moves between the two markets (e.g., Indian expat who was in NL, now in UK).

| Issue | Detail |
|---|---|
| ISA carried to NL | Included in Box 3 — NL doesn't recognise ISA wrapper |
| Dutch employer pension after leaving | Annuity paid from NL; taxable in NL or UK depending on DTAA |
| NL Box 3 vs UK CGT | On becoming UK resident, existing NL assets shift to UK CGT regime |

### Germany–Netherlands

| Issue | Detail |
|---|---|
| German ETF depot in NL | Included in Box 3 as foreign investment account |
| bAV pension | Complex. EU portability rights exist but impractical |
| Moving from DE to NL | German exit tax applies if ETF depot > €500k |

## SECTION 7: CROSS-BORDER INSIGHT FRAMING RULES

All cross-border insights generated by the AI layer must:

1. State the specific rule that applies (factual, not advice)
2. Identify which two countries are interacting
3. Name the relevant DTAA if applicable
4. End with: *"Cross-border tax rules are complex and specific to your situation — verify with a qualified cross-border tax advisor."*

Never:
- Compute actual tax liability
- Recommend whether to claim a relief or not (e.g., FIG)
- Say "you should" or "you should not"
- Estimate savings amounts
