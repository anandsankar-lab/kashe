## 1. THE THREE-BOX SYSTEM

The Netherlands taxes different income types in completely separate "boxes" with different rates.

| Box | What it covers | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Box 1 | Earned income, primary home (eigenwoningforfait + mortgage deduction) | 36.97% up to €75,518 / 49.5% above |
| Box 2 | Substantial interest (≥5% shareholding in a company) | 24.5% up to €67,804 / 33% above |
| Box 3 | Savings, investments, second home, crypto | 36% on assumed return above threshold |

Boxes are taxed independently. Losses in one box cannot offset income in another.

| Band | Rate |
|---|---|

**Heffingskortingen (tax credits):**
- Arbeidskorting (employment credit): reduces as income rises above ~€40k. Zero above ~€124k
- Algemene heffingskorting (general credit): €3,362 (2025). Reduces above €24,812
- These credits reduce actual tax paid significantly at lower incomes

## 3. BOX 3 — THE WEALTH TAX — COMPLETE PICTURE

### How Box 3 Works (2026)

Box 3 is NOT a capital gains tax. It is a **wealth tax** on assumed returns. You are taxed on what the government assumes your assets earned, not on what they actually earned.

**The calculation:**
1. Determine all Box 3 assets on January 1 (peildatum)
2. Subtract debts (with threshold: first €3,800 of debt is not deductible per person)
3. Subtract tax-free allowance: €59,357 per person (€118,714 for fiscal partners)
4. Apply assumed return rate per asset category
5. Tax = 36% × assumed return

**2026 assumed return rates:**

| Category | Assumed Return Rate 2026 |
|---|---|
| Bank balances and savings | ~1.44% (provisional — confirmed early 2026) |
| Investments and other assets | **7.78%** (FIXED — significantly higher than 2025) |
| Debts | ~2.62% (provisional) |

**Effective tax rates 2026:**
- Savings: 1.44% × 36% = **~0.52% effective wealth tax**
- Investments: 7.78% × 36% = **2.80% effective wealth tax**

### What is Included in Box 3

**YES — included:**
- Dutch bank and savings accounts
- Foreign bank and savings accounts (NRE, NRO, US savings, etc.)
- Stocks, ETFs, bonds (Dutch and foreign)
- Cryptocurrency
- Second homes and investment properties (Dutch and foreign)
- Foreign investment accounts (DeGiro, Zerodha, Vanguard US, etc.)
- Green investments: first €71,251 exempt (special regime)

**NO — excluded:**
- Primary residence (taxed in Box 1)
- Employer pension (pensioenfonds)
- Lijfrente annuity (until benefit paid)
- Business assets in active business (Box 1)
- US 401k / IRA (confirmed excluded for US persons)
- Pension assets: employer-initiated pension not under taxpayer's control

**SPECIAL RULES:**
- Cash above €672 per person counts as "investments" (not savings)
- Crypto: included under "investments and other assets"

### The OWR Route — Actual Return

If your actual return was LOWER than the assumed return:
- File an **Opgaaf Werkelijk Rendement (OWR)** with Belastingdienst
- Pay tax on actual return instead of assumed
- Result from 2021 Dutch Supreme Court ruling (Kerst ruling)
- This is NOT automatic — you must actively file

**Actual return includes:** interest received, dividends received, rental income, realised capital gains, unrealised gains (for investments)
**Important:** For 2026 and 2025, only realised gains count for investments (not unrealised) in the OWR calculation

### The Peildatum Rule

Box 3 is determined by a SINGLE snapshot on **January 1** each year. Value on January 2 or December 31 is irrelevant.

**Planning implication:** Reducing portfolio value before January 1 reduces Box 3 base. But this is complex — selling assets creates realisation events, cash above €672 still counts.

### The Tax-Free Allowance

| Year | Per Person | Fiscal Partners |
|---|---|---|
| 2025 | €57,684 | €115,368 |
| 2026 | €59,357 | €118,714 |

(Note: earlier drafts proposed reducing to €51,396 — this did NOT pass. Confirmed figure is €59,357.)

## 4. BOX 3 — 2028 REFORM (SENATE PENDING)

**Status as of April 2026:** Passed Dutch House of Representatives February 2026. Senate vote pending. Implementation from January 2028 if passed.

**What would change:**
- Tax on **actual** returns (not assumed)
- Includes unrealised capital gains (paper gains)
- 36% rate on actual return above €1,800 threshold
- Dividends, interest, rental income: taxed annually
- Investment appreciation: taxed annually even if not sold

**Asset exceptions in the 2028 proposal:**
- Real estate: capital gains taxed on realisation (not annually). Rental income taxed annually.
- Start-up shares: realisation basis

**If passed — implications for investors:**
- High-performing portfolios (>7.78% return): higher tax than current system
- Low-performing portfolios (<7.78% return): lower tax than current system
- Cash savers: minimal change (interest ~0-2% currently)
- Market corrections: no tax on paper losses carried forward indefinitely
- Loss carryforward: unlimited under proposed system

**For Kāshe:** Surface as "upcoming change" — not yet live. Monitor Senate vote.

## 5. INVESTMENT VEHICLES — COMPLETE REFERENCE

### Dutch Investment Depot (Effectenrekening / Beleggingsrekening)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Tax treatment | **Box 3** — wealth tax on assumed return. Not CGT. |
| Holding period | IRRELEVANT — no benefit to holding longer. Tax determined annually by value on Jan 1 |
| Threshold | First €59,357 per person: zero Box 3 tax |
| Above threshold | 2.80% effective annual wealth tax on investment assets |
| ETFs | Included in Box 3. Preferred over individual stocks for simplicity |
| Dividend | Subject to dividend tax withholding. Can claim back via annual return |
| GreenFunds (Groenfondsen) | First €71,251 exempt from Box 3. 0.7% heffingskorting |
| Foreign depot | Must declare. Included in Box 3. No difference from Dutch depot |

### Lijfrente (Annuity / Pension Savings)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Annual contribution limit | Age-dependent. Approximately 30% of pensionable income minus pension accrual |
| Tax treatment | Contribution: Box 1 deductible at marginal rate. Growth: not in Box 3. Payout: Box 1 income |
| Tax type | **EET** — Exempt contribution, Exempt growth, Taxed payout |
| Lock-in | Annuity must commence between ages 65-70 |
| Premature | Heavy revisierente (penalty tax ~20% extra) |
| Purpose | Best for self-employed without employer pension |

### Employer Pension (Pensioen via Werkgever)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Tax treatment | Employer and employee contributions: not taxed. Growth: not in Box 3. Payout: Box 1 |
| Mandatory | Most Dutch employees automatically enrolled |
| Not in Box 3 | Confirmed — employer pension assets excluded from Box 3 entirely |
| Cross-border | Portability: complex. EU pension portability rights exist but transfer is bureaucratic |
| Indian expat in NL | India EPF/NPS: complex. Get specialist advice on Dutch reporting obligations |

### AOW (State Pension)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Eligibility | Based on years of residence/work in Netherlands (1.96% per year, max 50 years) |
| Access age | 67 years (currently) |
| Amount (2026) | ~€1,400/month single / ~€970 each for couples |
| Expat note | Years outside NL: can voluntarily continue AOW contributions abroad |

### Savings Account (Spaarrekening)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Box 3 | Included. Assumed return: ~1.44% (provisional 2026). Effective tax: ~0.52% |
| Interest | Actual interest received. Current Dutch savings rates: ~2-3% p.a. |
| If actual return < assumed | File OWR to pay tax on actual lower return |
| Emergency fund | Up to €59,357 per person: zero Box 3 tax |

### Green Investments (Groenfondsen)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Box 3 exemption | First €71,251 per person (€142,502 fiscal partners) exempt from Box 3 |
| Additional credit | 0.7% heffingskorting on exempt amount (€499 max per person) |
| What qualifies | Certified green funds (ASN, Triodos, ABN AMRO MeesPierson green funds) |
| Yield | Typically lower than market returns (social/environmental premium) |

### Mortgage (Hypotheek)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Interest deductibility | Only annuity (annuïteit) or linear (lineair) mortgages qualify from 2013 |
| Interest-only (aflossingsvrij) | No deduction if taken after 2013. Large legacy portfolio |
| Maximum term for deduction | 30 years |
| Eigenwoningforfait | Imputed rental income taxed in Box 1 (currently 0.35% of WOZ value) |

## 6. THE 30% RULING

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| What it is | Tax-free expense reimbursement of 30% of salary for qualifying expats |
| Tapering (from 2024) | Year 1-2: 30%. Year 3-4: 20%. Year 5: 10% |
| Maximum duration | 5 years total |
| Salary threshold | €46,660/year (2025). €35,048 for under-30 with master's |
| Distance requirement | Must have lived >150km from Dutch border for 2 of last 24 months |
| Box 3 since Jan 2025 | **Partial non-resident status ended.** All worldwide assets included in Box 3 |
| Transitional | Had 30% ruling in 2023: partial non-resident exemption until 2026/2027 at latest |
| Impact for Indian expat | Indian MFs, NRE accounts, foreign portfolios: ALL now in Box 3 |

## 7. DIVIDEND WITHHOLDING TAX (DIVIDENDBELASTING)

- Standard rate: 15% withheld at source on Dutch company dividends
- Reclaim: file annual return to reclaim against Box 3 liability or get refund
- Foreign dividends: depends on DTA. US stocks: 15% US withholding tax. Credit available in NL.
- ETF distributions: typically handled within fund at fund level

## 8. PROPERTY

| Scenario | Tax treatment |
|---|---|
| Primary residence (eigenwoningforfait) | Box 1. Imputed income 0.35% of WOZ value. Mortgage interest deductible |
| Second home / investment property (NL) | Box 3. Annual wealth tax on 2.80% assumed return (investments category) |
| Second home / investment property (foreign) | Box 3. Same assumed return applies to worldwide real estate |
| Sale of primary residence | Capital gain: NOT taxed |
| Sale of investment property | Capital gain: included in Box 3 calculation on Jan 1 snapshot — NOT a separate CGT event |

**Under 2028 reform (Senate pending):**
- Rental income: taxed annually
- Property gains: taxed on realisation (not annually)
