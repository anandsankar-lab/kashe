## 1. INCOME TAX — RATES AND BANDS (England, Wales, Northern Ireland)

| Income | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Up to £12,570 | 0% | Personal Allowance |
| £12,571 – £50,270 | 20% | Basic rate |
| £50,271 – £125,140 | 40% | Higher rate |
| Above £125,140 | 45% | Additional rate |

**Personal Allowance taper:** Reduces by £1 for every £2 of income above £100,000. Lost entirely at £125,140.

**The 60% trap:** Effective 60% marginal rate on income between £100,000–£125,140 (income tax + PA loss). Pension contribution to bring income below £100k restores full PA.

**Scotland:** Different bands for earned and pension income (higher rates apply).

### Income Stacking Order — Critical for Investors

Income is stacked in this order to determine which rate band each type falls into:

1. Non-savings income (salary, rental, pension)
2. Savings income (interest)
3. Dividend income

**From April 2027:** Personal Allowance must be applied against earned income first, pushing savings and rental income into higher tax bands. Significant impact on investors with salary + investment income.

## 3. CAPITAL GAINS TAX

### Rates

| Taxpayer Band | Non-Residential Assets | Residential Property (non-main home) |
|---|---|---|
| Basic rate (after PA) | **18%** | **18%** |
| Higher/Additional rate | **24%** | **24%** |

Rates were aligned in Autumn Budget 2024. No long/short-term distinction in UK — CGT rate is the same regardless of how long you held an asset.

**Key exception:** Business Asset Disposal Relief (BADR) applies to qualifying disposals:
- 14% from April 2025 → 18% from April 2026
- £1 million lifetime limit
- Must be employee/officer with ≥5% shareholding for ≥2 years

### CGT Calculation Rules

- Net gains = total gains − total losses in the year
- Deduct Annual Exempt Amount (£3,000)
- Gains stacked on top of income to determine rate
- Unused AEA: cannot carry forward
- Losses: can carry forward indefinitely

### Bed and ISA Strategy

Sell holding in GIA, realise gain (use up AEA), repurchase inside ISA. Resets cost basis. Most effective when gains are close to the £3,000 AEA. Requires selling and rebuying — price risk during transition.

### Spousal Transfer

Transfers between spouses/civil partners: no CGT at time of transfer. Each partner has their own AEA and rate bands. Effective strategy: transfer appreciated asset to lower-earning spouse before sale.

## 9. FIG REGIME — FOREIGN INCOME AND GAINS (from April 2025)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Eligibility | Must be UK resident + within first 4 tax years of UK residence + 10+ years non-UK prior |
| Benefit | Foreign income and gains NOT taxable in UK during FIG years |
| Trade-off | **Lose Personal Allowance AND CGT Annual Exempt Amount** if FIG claimed |
| Also lose | Blind person's allowance, transferable tax allowance, foreign capital/income losses |
| Years available | 4 tax years from year of arrival (or from 2025/26 if arrived 2022/23–2025/26) |
| Remittance | Foreign income/gains relieved under FIG can be brought to UK tax-free |
| Planning note | For Indian nationals new to UK: Indian MF gains, NRE interest, rental income from India may be FIG-exempt in first 4 years |
| Deadline to claim | 31 January after end of relevant tax year |
| NRE accounts | Interest is tax-free in India but TAXABLE in UK under arising basis. FIG may exempt during first 4 years. After FIG: taxable at up to 40% with NO FTC (India didn't tax it) |

### Critical NRE Account Trap for UK Residents

NRE interest is exempt in India. The UK-India DTAA provides "tax sparing" credit (UK credits tax as if India charged 15%) — but this is time-limited and complex to claim. After the FIG window, UK residents pay full UK income tax on NRE interest with no credit. A higher-rate UK taxpayer pays 40% on what is effectively zero-taxed income in India.
