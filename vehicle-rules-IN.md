## 1. INCOME TAX — RATES AND BANDS

### New Tax Regime (Default from FY 2024-25)

| Income Slab | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹4,00,000 | NIL |
| ₹4,00,001 – ₹8,00,000 | 5% |
| ₹8,00,001 – ₹12,00,000 | 10% |
| ₹12,00,001 – ₹16,00,000 | 15% |
| ₹16,00,001 – ₹20,00,000 | 20% |
| ₹20,00,001 – ₹24,00,000 | 25% |
| Above ₹24,00,000 | 30% |

**Section 87A rebate (new regime):** Up to ₹60,000 if total income ≤ ₹12,00,000. Does NOT apply to capital gains at special rates (Sections 111A, 112, 112A).

**Standard deduction (salaried):** ₹75,000. Effective zero-tax salary threshold: ₹12,75,000.

**Surcharge:**
- Income > ₹50L: 10%
- Income > ₹1Cr: 15%
- Income > ₹2Cr: 25%
- Income > ₹5Cr: 25% (capped — marginal relief applies)

**Health & Education Cess:** 4% on tax + surcharge.

### Old Tax Regime (Opt-in via Form 10-IEA)

| Income Slab | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹2,50,000 | NIL |
| ₹2,50,001 – ₹5,00,000 | 5% |
| ₹5,00,001 – ₹10,00,000 | 20% |
| Above ₹10,00,000 | 30% |

**Section 87A rebate (old regime):** Up to ₹12,500 if total income ≤ ₹5,00,000.

**Key advantage of old regime:** Full deduction ecosystem (80C, 80D, HRA, LTA, home loan interest, etc.).

## 2. CAPITAL GAINS — COMPLETE RATE TABLE

### The July 23, 2024 Inflection Point
The Finance (No. 2) Act 2024 changed rates for transfers on or after July 23, 2024. All rates below reflect the post-July 23, 2024 regime unless noted.

### Equity — Listed Shares, Equity MFs, ELSS (after lock-in)

| Holding Period | Classification | Rate | Key Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| < 12 months | Short-term (STCG) | **20%** (Section 111A) | Was 15% pre-July 2024 |
| ≥ 12 months | Long-term (LTCG) | **12.5%** above ₹1.25L annual exemption (Section 112A) | Was 10% above ₹1L pre-July 2024 |

**Critical:** The ₹1.25L annual exemption under 112A is per financial year across all equity LTCG. It cannot be transferred to future years.

**Section 87A trap:** Even if total income < ₹12L, the 20% STCG and 12.5% LTCG are payable. The rebate cannot offset special-rate taxes.

### Property — Immovable Property (Land, Building, House)

| Holding Period | Classification | Rate | Options |
|---|---|---|---|
| < 24 months | Short-term | Slab rate (up to 30%) | No indexation |
| ≥ 24 months (acquired before July 23, 2024) | Long-term | **12.5% without indexation OR 20% with indexation** | Choose whichever is lower |
| ≥ 24 months (acquired on/after July 23, 2024) | Long-term | **12.5% without indexation only** | No indexation available |

**TDS on property sale:** Buyer deducts TDS at 12.5% (LTCG) or slab rate (STCG). NRI sellers: TDS at 20% (LTCG) or 30% (STCG). File ITR to claim refund if excess TDS.

**NRI note:** Cannot claim basic exemption limit against property LTCG. Residents can.

### Debt Mutual Funds

| Acquired | Rate | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Before April 1, 2023 | 12.5% LTCG if held > 36 months; slab if < 36 months | Indexation available for pre-July 23, 2024 acquisition (if held for LT) |
| On/after April 1, 2023 | **Slab rate regardless of holding period** | Section 50AA — no LTCG benefit ever |

**Market-linked debentures (MLDs):** Always taxed as STCG at slab rate, no holding period benefit.

### Gold, Silver, Sovereign Gold Bonds

| Asset | Holding | Rate | Special Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| Physical gold/silver | < 24 months | Slab rate | — |
| Physical gold/silver | ≥ 24 months | 12.5% without indexation | — |
| Gold ETF / Gold MF | < 12 months | Slab rate | Treated as non-equity fund |
| Gold ETF / Gold MF | ≥ 12 months | 12.5% | Treated as listed |
| **SGB held to maturity (8 years)** | ≥ 8 years | **TAX FREE** | Section 10(15)(iv)(h) — complete exemption |
| SGB sold before maturity (year 5+) | ≥ 24 months | 12.5% | Can sell on exchange after year 5 |

### Cryptocurrency and Virtual Digital Assets (VDAs)

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Tax rate | **30% flat regardless of holding period** |
| Deductions | Only cost of acquisition — no other deductions |
| Loss offset | Cannot offset against any other income head |
| Loss carry-forward | Cannot carry forward crypto losses |
| TDS on sale | 1% TDS on transactions > ₹10,000 (₹50,000 for specified persons) |
| Gifting | VDA gifts taxable at 30% in hands of recipient |

### Other Asset Classes

| Asset | Short-term period | Short-term rate | Long-term rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unlisted shares | < 24 months | Slab rate | 12.5% without indexation |
| Bonds/Debentures (listed) | < 12 months | Slab rate | 12.5% |
| Bonds/Debentures (unlisted) | < 36 months | Slab rate | 12.5% |
| REITs/InvITs units | < 12 months | 20% | 12.5% above ₹1.25L |
| Foreign equity (via LRS) | < 24 months | Slab rate | 12.5% |

### Capital Loss Offset Rules

```
Short-Term Capital Loss (STCL):
  → Can offset STCG from any asset class
  → Can offset LTCG from any asset class
  → CANNOT offset salary, interest, or other income heads

Long-Term Capital Loss (LTCL):
  → Can ONLY offset LTCG
  → CANNOT offset STCG
  → CANNOT offset salary, interest, or other income heads

Crypto losses:
  → CANNOT offset any other income or gain — not even other crypto gains

Carry-forward:
  → Up to 8 assessment years
  → Must file ITR within due date to preserve carry-forward right
```

## 3. REINVESTMENT EXEMPTIONS — SECTIONS 54, 54F, 54EC

### Section 54 — Property Sale → New Residential Property

| Parameter | Rule |
|---|---|
| Trigger asset | Sale of residential house property (LTCG only) |
| Investment required | Buy or construct residential house in India |
| Time limit (purchase) | 1 year before OR 2 years after sale date |
| Time limit (construction) | 3 years from sale date |
| Maximum exemption | **₹10 crore** (AY 2024-25 onwards) |
| Properties allowed | Up to 2 houses (if LTCG ≤ ₹2 crore) |
| Lock-in | Must hold new property ≥ 3 years |
| CGAS | If reinvestment incomplete before ITR filing: park in Capital Gains Account Scheme |

### Section 54F — Any LTCG → New Residential Property

| Parameter | Rule |
|---|---|
| Trigger asset | Sale of ANY long-term asset EXCEPT residential house |
| Investment required | Invest **entire net sale proceeds** (not just gain) in residential house |
| Proportionate exemption | If partial investment: exemption = (amount invested / net consideration) × gain |
| Time limit | Same as Section 54 |
| Maximum exemption | **₹10 crore** |
| Restriction | Must not own > 1 other house on date of transfer |
| Lock-in | Must hold new property ≥ 3 years |

**Key difference from 54:** 54F requires investing net sale proceeds, not just the gain.

### Section 54EC — Property LTCG → Specified Bonds

| Parameter | Rule |
|---|---|
| Trigger asset | LTCG from sale of land or building only |
| Investment required | NHAI bonds or REC bonds (specified bonds) |
| Investment cap | **₹50 lakh per financial year** |
| Time limit | Within 6 months of sale date |
| Lock-in | **5 years** (premature redemption cancels exemption) |
| CGAS | Not required — direct bond investment |

**Critical distinction:** 54EC is capped at ₹50L — far lower than 54/54F's ₹10Cr cap. Useful for smaller property gains only.

### Capital Gains Account Scheme (CGAS)

If you've sold an asset and intend to reinvest under Section 54/54F but haven't completed reinvestment before ITR filing deadline (July 31):
- Deposit unutilised capital gains in CGAS account at authorised PSU bank
- Protects exemption eligibility while reinvestment is underway
- Must be used within the time limits of the respective section
- Unused amount after time limit → treated as capital gain in that year

## 4. INVESTMENT VEHICLES — COMPLETE REFERENCE

### PPF (Public Provident Fund)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Annual limit | ₹1,50,000 |
| Regime | Old regime: 80C deduction. New regime: NO deduction |
| Tax treatment | **EEE** — Exempt on contribution, Exempt on growth, Exempt on withdrawal |
| Primary lock-in | **15 years** from opening date |
| Partial withdrawal | From year 7 onwards (up to 50% of balance at end of 4th year) |
| Extension | In 5-year blocks after 15-year maturity |
| Premature closure | From year 5 (1% interest penalty) |
| Interest rate | Government-declared quarterly (currently ~7.1%) |
| NRI access | **NRIs cannot open new PPF accounts.** Existing accounts allowed until maturity at lower non-resident rate. Cannot extend after maturity. |
| Cross-border flag | UK residents: PPF interest taxable in UK (no FTC available — India exempts it). NL: included in Box 3. |

### ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Annual limit | ₹1,50,000 (under 80C, old regime only) |
| Tax treatment | Contribution: 80C deduction (old regime). Growth: LTCG rules apply after lock-in. |
| Lock-in | **3 years** — mandatory, no premature exit |
| Post-lock-in | LTCG at 12.5% above ₹1.25L annual exemption |
| Dividend | Taxable at slab rate (IDCW) |
| New regime | ELSS contribution gives NO deduction. But gains still taxed as equity LTCG after lock-in |
| NRI access | NRIs can hold ELSS (must have valid NRE/NRO account and KYC) |
| Holding period alert | Fire at 33 months (approaching 3-year unlock) |

### NPS (National Pension System)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Tier 1 annual limit | No upper limit on contribution |
| Regime benefit | 80CCD(1): Up to ₹1.5L (within 80C) — old regime only. 80CCD(1B): Additional ₹50,000 — old regime only. **80CCD(2): Employer contribution — deductible under BOTH regimes** |
| Employer contribution | Up to 10% of salary (14% for central govt employees) — no cap in absolute terms |
| Lock-in | **Until age 60** |
| Partial withdrawal | After 3 years: up to 25% for specified purposes (child education, marriage, house purchase, illness) |
| At retirement (60) | Minimum 40% must be used to purchase annuity. Up to 60% lump sum: **TAX FREE** |
| Annuity income | Taxable as income at slab rate |
| NRI access | Yes, with NRE/NRO account |
| Cross-border flag | US persons: NPS is NOT a PFIC (defined benefit pension). UK: complex — not recognised as pension by HMRC. Germany: taxable on contributions as income. |

### EPF (Employee Provident Fund)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Contribution | Employee: 12% of basic salary. Employer: 12% (3.67% EPF + 8.33% EPS) |
| Tax on employee contribution | 80C deduction (old regime). Interest: tax-free up to ₹2.5L contribution (₹5L for govt employees) |
| Interest above threshold | Interest on contributions > ₹2.5L taxable at slab rate |
| Lock-in | Continuous employment. Withdrawal on retirement, resignation after 5 years (or medical/other specified reasons) |
| Tax on withdrawal | Tax-free if service ≥ 5 years. Taxable if < 5 years (with TDS) |
| NRI | EPF continues during NRI period. Transfer to NPS on return or withdraw |

### Fixed Deposits (FDs)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Tax-saver FD | 5-year lock-in. 80C deduction (old regime only). Interest: **taxable at slab rate annually** |
| Regular FD | No lock-in restrictions. Interest: taxable at slab rate annually (not just at maturity) |
| TDS | 10% TDS if interest > ₹40,000 per year per bank (₹50,000 for senior citizens). Submit Form 15G/15H to avoid TDS if income below taxable threshold |
| Premature | Penalty (typically 0.5–1% interest penalty) |
| Holding period alert | Fire 60 days before maturity date |
| NRE FD | **Interest: tax-free in India**. Lock-in: 1 year minimum. Cross-border: taxable in UK/NL/US/DE |
| NRO FD | Interest: taxable in India at 30% TDS. DTAA may reduce rate |
| FCNR | Foreign currency. Interest: tax-free in India. No exchange rate risk |

### Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGBs)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Annual limit | 4 kg per individual per fiscal year |
| Issue price | RBI-declared (linked to gold price) |
| Interest | 2.5% p.a. on issue price. Taxable at slab rate annually |
| Maturity period | **8 years** |
| Redemption before maturity | RBI window: year 5 onwards. Exchange: tradeable on NSE/BSE |
| **Tax at maturity (8 years)** | **COMPLETELY TAX-FREE** — Section 10(15)(iv)(h) |
| Tax on pre-maturity exchange sale | LTCG 12.5% if held > 24 months |
| NRI | NRIs cannot purchase new SGBs. Can hold existing SGBs |
| Cross-border | UK/NL/US/DE residents: gains may be taxable in residence country even if tax-free in India |

### Equity Mutual Funds (non-ELSS)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| STCG (< 12 months) | 20% |
| LTCG (≥ 12 months) | 12.5% above ₹1.25L annual exemption |
| Dividend (IDCW) | Taxable at slab rate |
| SIP taxation | Each SIP instalment is a separate purchase with its own 12-month clock |
| Fund switching | Treated as redemption + repurchase — taxable event |
| NRI TDS | 20% TDS on STCG. 12.5% TDS on LTCG. File ITR for refund if excess TDS |

### Debt Mutual Funds (acquired from April 1, 2023)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Tax rate | **Slab rate regardless of holding period** — Section 50AA |
| Indexation | Not available |
| Old debt MFs (pre-April 1, 2023) | LTCG (> 36 months): 12.5%; STCG: slab rate |
| Use case | Liquid funds, ultra-short duration: still useful for parking, returns taxed as income |

### Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) / Infrastructure Investment Trusts (InvITs)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Listed REIT/InvIT units | STCG < 12 months: 20%. LTCG ≥ 12 months: 12.5% above ₹1.25L |
| Dividends from REIT | Partially taxable (depends on REIT structure — SPV vs direct) |
| Interest component | Taxable at slab rate |

### ULIPs (Unit Linked Insurance Plans)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Lock-in | **5 years** minimum |
| Tax exemption | Section 10(10D): Maturity proceeds tax-free IF annual premium ≤ ₹2.5L (for policies issued after Feb 1, 2021) |
| Premium > ₹2.5L | Proceeds taxable as capital gains (LTCG rates apply) |
| 80C | Premium deductible (old regime) if ≤ 10% of sum assured |
| NRI | Can hold; maturity proceeds repatriable via NRO account |

### NSC (National Savings Certificate)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Annual limit | No upper limit |
| Lock-in | **5 years** |
| Interest | Taxable at slab rate (accrued annually, reinvested) |
| 80C | Investment deductible up to ₹1.5L (old regime) |
| NRI | NRIs cannot invest in new NSC. Existing investments allowed until maturity |

### SCSS (Senior Citizens Savings Scheme)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Age eligibility | 60+ (55+ for retired defence personnel) |
| Annual limit | ₹30,00,000 per individual |
| Tenure | 5 years (extendable by 3 years once) |
| Interest | Government-declared quarterly. Taxable at slab rate. TDS if interest > ₹50,000 |
| 80C | Investment deductible (old regime) |
| NRI | **NRIs not eligible** |

### RBI Floating Rate Savings Bonds (FRSB)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Lock-in | **7 years** |
| Interest | Linked to NSC rate + 0.35%. Taxable at slab rate. Paid semi-annually |
| Limit | No upper limit |
| 80C | NOT eligible for 80C deduction |
| Premature | Allowed for senior citizens after 4 years (60-70 age) or 5 years (70-80) or 6 years (80+) |

### Section 80C — Complete Deduction Ecosystem (Old Regime Only)

**Shared limit: ₹1,50,000 per financial year**

Instruments within the ₹1.5L cap:
- PPF contributions
- ELSS investments
- NSC investments
- Tax-saver FD (5-year)
- ULIP premium (if ≤ 10% of sum assured)
- Life insurance premium
- EPF employee contribution
- NPS Tier 1 under 80CCD(1)
- Home loan principal repayment
- Children's tuition fees
- Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana

**Additional deductions (old regime):**
- 80CCD(1B): Additional ₹50,000 for NPS Tier 1 (beyond 80C limit)
- **80CCD(2): Employer NPS contribution — deductible under BOTH regimes (no cap in percentage terms)**
- 80D: Health insurance premium (₹25,000 individual + ₹25,000 parents; ₹50,000 for senior citizen parents)
- 80TTA: ₹10,000 interest from savings account (not FDs)
- 80TTB: ₹50,000 interest from all deposits for senior citizens (60+) — replaces 80TTA

## 6. NRI-SPECIFIC RULES

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Residency determination | NRI if < 182 days in India in FY. RNOR: returned but < 730 days in last 7 years |
| NRE account | Interest: tax-free in India. Fully repatriable |
| NRO account | Interest: taxable at 30% TDS (DTAA may reduce). Repatriation: up to $1M/year |
| FCNR account | Foreign currency. Interest: tax-free in India |
| Investments prohibited | New PPF, NSC, SCSS, SSY, KVP, RBI Savings Bonds |
| 87A rebate | **NRIs not eligible** |
| Basic exemption vs CGT | NRIs cannot apply basic exemption limit against capital gains (residents can) |
| TDS on gains | Deducted at source — file ITR to claim refund |
| DTAA | Reduces withholding rates. Requires: Tax Residency Certificate (TRC) + Form 10F + PAN |
