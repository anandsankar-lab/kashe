Standard deduction 2026: $16,100 single / $32,200 MFJ / $24,150 head of household.

**US taxation by citizenship:** The United States taxes citizens and green card holders on **worldwide income regardless of where they live**. This is unique globally. DTAA saving clauses prevent treaty-based escape from US tax.

## 2. CAPITAL GAINS TAX — COMPLETE PICTURE

### Long-Term Capital Gains (held > 12 months)

| Taxable Income — Single | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to $49,450 | **0%** |
| $49,451 – $544,925 | **15%** |
| Above $544,925 | **20%** |

| Taxable Income — Married Filing Jointly | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to $98,900 | 0% |
| $98,901 – $614,900 | 15% |
| Above $614,900 | 20% |

**Rates are inflation-adjusted annually. Thresholds based on TOTAL taxable income, not just capital gains.**

Capital gains are stacked on top of ordinary income. Example: $70k salary + $30k LTCG = $100k total → the LTCG portion that pushes above $49,450 is taxed at 15%.

### Short-Term Capital Gains (held ≤ 12 months)

Taxed as ordinary income at your marginal rate. Same brackets as salary. Up to 37% federal.

**The 12-month threshold is the single most impactful holding period rule in US investing.** Holding 11 months vs 13 months can mean the difference between 37% and 20% on the same gain.

### Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT) — 3.8%

Additional 3.8% on investment income for high earners:

| Filing Status | MAGI Threshold |
|---|---|
| Single | $200,000 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $250,000 |
| Married Filing Separately | $125,000 |

**Critical: These thresholds are NOT inflation-adjusted.** Set by Congress in 2010 and unchanged. More taxpayers caught every year as incomes rise.

NIIT applies to the LESSER of: (a) net investment income, or (b) amount MAGI exceeds threshold.

Applies to: capital gains, qualified dividends, interest, rents, royalties, passive business income.

**Maximum effective federal CGT rate: 20% + 3.8% = 23.8%**

### Special Rate Assets

| Asset Type | Maximum CGT Rate | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Collectibles (art, coins, precious metals, antiques) | 28% | Regardless of income level |
| Section 1250 depreciation recapture (real estate) | 25% | On previously depreciated amount |
| Qualified Small Business Stock (Section 1202) | 0%–28% | Up to 100% exclusion if conditions met |

## 3. QUALIFIED DIVIDENDS

Qualified dividends taxed at same 0%/15%/20% rates as LTCG.

**Requirements for qualified treatment:**
- Paid by US corporation or qualified foreign corporation
- Stock held >60 days during 121-day window around ex-dividend date

Ordinary (non-qualified) dividends: taxed as income up to 37%. Includes money market funds, most REITs, some foreign stocks.

## 5. INVESTMENT VEHICLES — COMPLETE REFERENCE

### 401(k) — Traditional

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Employee contribution limit | **$23,500** (2026). Age 50+: +$7,500 catch-up = $31,000 |
| Employer match | Does NOT count toward employee limit. Employers commonly match 50-100% up to 6% |
| Tax treatment | **EET** — contributions pre-tax, growth tax-deferred, withdrawals taxed as ordinary income |
| ROTH 401k option | Same limits. Contributions post-tax. Withdrawals tax-free |
| RMD age | 73 (SECURE 2.0) |
| Early withdrawal | 10% penalty + income tax if < 59½ (with exceptions) |
| Employer match | **This is the highest guaranteed return available to any investor.** 50% match = immediate 50% return. Never leave it unclaimed |
| Cross-border | NL: excluded from Box 3. UK: complex — HMRC may not recognise 401k as pension. Germany: not recognised as pension by German tax law |

### Roth 401(k)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Contribution limit | Same as traditional 401k ($23,500 / $31,000 age 50+) |
| Tax treatment | **TEE** — post-tax contributions, tax-free growth, tax-free qualified withdrawals |
| No RMDs | From account holder — unlike traditional 401k |
| 5-year rule | Earnings withdrawable tax-free after 5 years AND age 59½ |

### IRA — Traditional

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Annual limit | **$7,000** ($8,000 age 50+) |
| Deductibility | Fully deductible if no workplace plan. Phase-out if workplace plan: single $79k–$89k; MFJ $126k–$146k |
| Tax treatment | EET — pre-tax contribution (if deductible), tax-deferred growth, income on withdrawal |
| RMD | Age 73 |

### Roth IRA

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Annual limit | $7,000 ($8,000 age 50+) |
| Income limit | Phase-out: single $150k–$165k; MFJ $236k–$246k. Above limit: backdoor Roth |
| Tax treatment | **TEE** — post-tax, tax-free growth, tax-free qualified withdrawals |
| No RMDs | During account holder's lifetime |
| Backdoor Roth | Convert non-deductible Traditional IRA → Roth IRA. Complex if existing IRA basis |
| Cross-border | UK: complex. Some HMRC guidance treats Roth as taxable. Germany: not recognised |

### Health Savings Account (HSA)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| 2026 limits | Individual: $4,300. Family: $8,550. Age 55+: +$1,000 |
| Tax treatment | **Triple tax-advantaged:** pre-tax contributions, tax-free growth, tax-free qualified medical withdrawals |
| After 65 | Non-medical withdrawals taxed as income (like IRA) — no penalty |
| Requirement | Must be enrolled in High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) |
| Investment | Most HSAs allow investment after minimum balance threshold (~$1,000) |

### 529 College Savings Plan

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Annual limit | No federal limit (but gift tax annual exclusion applies: $19,000/year) |
| Contribution | Post-tax. State deduction in many states |
| Growth | Tax-free if used for qualified education expenses |
| Unused funds | Can roll into Roth IRA (up to $35,000 lifetime, after 15 years) |

### General Investment Account (GIA / Brokerage Account)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Tax treatment | Taxed — capital gains on realisation, dividends as income |
| LTCG | Hold > 12 months for preferential rates |
| 0% rate harvesting | If income < $49,450 (single): realise LTCG tax-free. Reset cost basis |
| Loss harvesting | Sell losers to offset winners. Up to $3,000 loss offset against ordinary income |
| Wash-sale rule | Cannot repurchase substantially identical security within 30 days before/after loss sale |

### I Bonds (Inflation-Protected Savings Bonds)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Annual limit | $10,000 per person per year (plus $5,000 via tax refund) |
| Tax treatment | Federal: deferred until redemption. State: exempt |
| Interest rate | Fixed rate + inflation adjustment (variable) — currently competitive |
| Access | Cannot redeem < 12 months. 12–60 months: 3-month interest penalty. ≥ 60 months: no penalty |
| Holding period alert | Fire at 10 months (approaching 12-month minimum) |
| Education benefit | Interest potentially tax-free if used for qualified education expenses |

## 6. HOME SALE EXCLUSION — SECTION 121

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Exclusion amount | $250,000 single / $500,000 MFJ |
| Requirements | Own the property AND use as primary residence for 2 of last 5 years |
| Frequency | Can use every 2 years |
| Gain above exclusion | Subject to capital gains tax at LTCG rates + NIIT if applicable |
| Cross-border expat | If property sold after establishing foreign residency, confirm US residency use period. Partial exclusion available if requirements partially met |

## 7. 1031 EXCHANGE — REAL ESTATE

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Purpose | Defer CGT on investment property sale by rolling into new property |
| Identification | Must identify replacement property within 45 days of sale |
| Closing | Must close on replacement within 180 days |
| Asset type | Investment or business property only. Not primary residence |
| Tax basis | Carries over (deferred, not eliminated) |
| Death | Stepped-up basis at death eliminates deferred gain entirely |

## 8. TAX-LOSS HARVESTING

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Offset | Capital losses offset capital gains dollar-for-dollar |
| Ordinary income offset | Net capital losses up to $3,000/year can offset ordinary income |
| Carry-forward | Unlimited carry-forward for unused losses |
| Wash-sale rule | Cannot buy substantially identical security 30 days before or after the loss sale. Wash sale disallows the loss. |
| Cross-fund harvesting | Sell S&P 500 ETF, buy total market ETF — different enough to avoid wash-sale |

## 9. PFIC — PASSIVE FOREIGN INVESTMENT COMPANY

This is the most critical cross-border tax issue for US persons with Indian investments.

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| What qualifies as PFIC | Non-US mutual fund, ETF, or pooled investment vehicle |
| **Indian MFs: PFIC?** | **YES** — Indian mutual funds are PFICs for US persons |
| **Indian stocks: PFIC?** | **NO** — individual Indian stocks are NOT PFICs |
| **Indian bonds/FDs: PFIC?** | **NO** — not PFICs |
| **Indian ETFs: PFIC?** | **YES** — same as mutual funds |
| **PPF, EPF, NPS: PFIC?** | **NO** — not PFICs |
| Filing requirement | Form 8621 annually for each PFIC. Expensive and complex. |
| Tax treatment | Gains taxed at highest ordinary income rate + interest charges for prior years |
| Threshold | $25,000 (single) / $50,000 (MFJ) for unsold PFIC assets — below threshold may avoid filing |
| US-listed Indian ETFs | May avoid PFIC (e.g., iShares INDA) — check fund structure |

**Key insight for Kāshe:** If user is US person AND holds Indian MFs → trigger PFIC warning immediately. This is not a minor issue — penalties can be severe.

## 10. FOREIGN TAX CREDIT (FTC)

US persons can credit foreign taxes paid against US tax liability to avoid double taxation.

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Credit vs deduction | Credit is generally better than deduction |
| Form 1116 | Required for FTC claim |
| Limitation | FTC cannot exceed US tax on that income |
| DTAA interaction | Saving clause means US citizens cannot use treaty to avoid US tax |
| India DTAA | Credit available for Indian CGT paid. But PFIC gains: ordinary income rate — FTC may not fully offset |
| Foreign Earned Income Exclusion | Up to $126,500 (2024) earned income abroad excluded — but does NOT apply to investment income |

## 11. FBAR AND FATCA — FOREIGN ACCOUNT REPORTING

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| FBAR (FinCEN 114) | Report foreign bank accounts if total exceeds $10,000 at any point in year |
| FATCA (Form 8938) | Report foreign financial assets. Thresholds: $50k single (in US) / $200k abroad |
| Foreign mutual funds | Must report on FBAR and potentially Form 8938 |
| PPF, NRE, NRO accounts | Must report on FBAR |
| Penalties | Wilful non-filing: up to $100k or 50% of account value per year |
